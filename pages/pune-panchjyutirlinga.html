Bhimashankar, Pune

Bhimashankar is known to be one of the holiest and most ancient holy shrines of Shiva. It is surrounded by verdant, scenic forests and lush areas. These forests have been declared as the Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary. Here, Lord Shiva is portrayed in the form of half woman and half man (Ardhanarishwar). Trimbakeshwar, Nashik

This one is considered as a major Jyotirlinga among all twelve jyotirlongas in India. It is also known to be the origin of Godavari River. Considering different directions at the location of this jyotirlinga the sun, the moon and Agni, make up the three eyes. The three faced lingam is known to be the most unique and fascinating aspect about this temple. These three faces represent Lord Brahma, Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva.

Parli Vaijnath Temple

Parli Vaijnath Temple, also known as Shri Vaijnath Temple, is a revered Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in Parli, Maharashtra. It is recognized as one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, which are considered the most sacred abodes of Lord Shiva. ​

Historical Significance:
The temple's origins are ancient, with some beliefs suggesting it dates back approximately 3,000 years. It was renovated in the 1700s by Rani Ahilyabai Holkar, a prominent Maratha queen known for her contributions to temple restorations across India.

Grishneshwar, Aurangabad

Grishneshwar Temple, located in the village of Verul near Aurangabad, Maharashtra, is one of the twelve revered Jyotirlinga shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva. The name 'Grishneshwar' translates to 'Lord of Compassion'. ​

Historical Significance:
The temple has a rich history marked by cycles of destruction and reconstruction. Initially destroyed during the Delhi Sultanate's rule in the 13th and 14th centuries, it was first rebuilt in the 16th century by Maloji Bhosale, the grandfather of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Later, in 1729, it was restored to its current form by Queen Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore.

Aundha Nagnath Temple

Aundha Nagnath Temple, located in Aundha Nagnath, Maharashtra, is a revered Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is considered one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, which are sacred representations of Lord Shiva. ​

Historical Significance:
The temple's origins are believed to date back to the time of the Mahabharata, with legends attributing its construction to Yudhishthira, the eldest of the Pandavas, during their exile. The current structure is said to have been built in the 13th century by the Seuna (Yadava) dynasty. Historically, the temple suffered destruction during Aurangzeb's conquests and was later rebuilt by Ahilyabai Holkar. ​
